About

From neural dynamics to conscious experience

I am a cognitive neuroscientist, Senior Researcher, and Assistant PI in Human Neuroethology at Freie Universität Berlin. My research investigates the neural mechanisms of conscious perception, with a particular focus on brain oscillations, sensory entrainment, and individual variability.

I combine psychophysics with EEG, MEG, fMRI, intracranial recordings, computational modelling, and non-invasive brain stimulation. More recently, I have been developing neuroethological and naturalistic approaches that place conscious experience within its biological, behavioral, and ecological context.

Research

Three connected research directions

01

Conscious perception

I investigate how subjective experience relates to behavior and neural activity across vision and audition, including residual visual processing following V1 damage.

02

Neural rhythms and causal stimulation

I study how endogenous oscillations, rhythmic sensory input, and transcranial alternating current stimulation influence perception and behavior.

03

Human neuroethology

I develop approaches that connect mechanisms of consciousness with development, function, evolution, bodily state, and behavior in more naturalistic contexts.
